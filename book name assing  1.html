1.The Three Musketeers

Alexandre Dumas

description:

First published in 1844, it follows the adventures of a young man named d'Artagnan who travels to Paris to join the Musketeers of the Guard. There, he befriends three inseparable musketeers: Athos, Porthos, and Aramis, and together they embark on various adventures full of intrigue, romance, and swashbuckling action. Set in 17th-century France during the reign of King Louis XIII, the novel is renowned for its exciting plot, memorable characters, and themes of loyalty, honor, and friendship.

2.The call of wild

jack london

description:

"The Call of the Wild" is a novel written by American author Jack London. Published in 1903, it tells the story of Buck, a domestic dog who is stolen from his comfortable home in California and sold into the harsh life of an Alaskan sled dog during the Klondike Gold Rush of the 1890s. As Buck adapts to the brutal realities of the wilderness, he undergoes a transformation, tapping into his primal instincts and ancestral memories to become a dominant leader among the sled dogs.

First published in 1870, the novel is renowned for its imaginative exploration of underwater worlds and advanced technology for its time. As they embark on a journey beneath the ocean, they encounter a variety of wonders and dangers, from exotic marine life to treacherous underwater landscapes.

5.Sila Nerangalil Sila Manithargal

jeyakanthan

description:

"Sila Nerangalil Sila Manithargal" is a Tamil novel written by Jeyakanthan, a prominent writer in the Tamil literary world. First published in 1970, the title translates to "Some Men and Some Women" in English. The novel delves into the complexities of human relationships, societal norms, and the struggles faced by individuals, particularly women, in traditional Indian society. The narrative revolves around the protagonist, Valli, a young woman who works as a typist in a male-dominated office environment. Through Valli's experiences and interactions with various characters, the novel explores themes of gender discrimination, sexual harassment, societal expectations, and the quest for personal freedom and fulfillment.

6.the great gatsby

F. Scott Fitzgerald

description:

Set in the summer of 1922, the story takes place in the fictional town of West Egg on Long Island, New York, and is narrated by Nick Carraway, a Yale graduate and World War I veteran. The novel primarily revolves around Jay Gatsby, a mysterious and enigmatic millionaire who throws extravagant parties at his mansion. As Nick becomes entangled in the lives of his wealthy and privileged neighbors, he learns about Gatsby's obsessive pursuit of Daisy Buchanan, Nick's cousin and the love of Gatsby's life. Daisy is married to Tom Buchanan, a wealthy and arrogant man who represents the established social elite. Through themes of love, wealth, ambition, and the American Dream, "The Great Gatsby" explores the decadence and moral decay of the Jazz Age, reflecting on the superficiality and emptiness that often underlie the pursuit of wealth and status. Fitzgerald's prose is celebrated for its lyrical beauty and evocative descriptions, capturing the essence of the Roaring Twenties and the disillusionment of the post-war generation.

7.the silent patient

Alex Michaelides

description:

"The Silent Patient" is a psychological thriller novel written by Alex Michaelides, published in 2019. The story revolves around Alicia Berenson, a renowned painter who seemingly has a perfect life until she shoots her husband Gabriel five times in the face and then stops speaking. Alicia is subsequently admitted to a psychiatric facility called The Grove, where she becomes known as the "silent patient." The narrative is primarily told from the perspective of Theo Faber, a psychotherapist who becomes obsessed with Alicia's case. Determined to uncover the truth behind her silence and the motive for the murder, Theo takes on Alicia's therapy at The Grove. As Theo delves deeper into Alicia's past and psyche, he unravels a complex web of secrets, betrayals, and hidden traumas.

8.the lord of the rings

J.R.R. Tolkien

description:

The Lord of the Rings" is a high fantasy epic novel written by J.R.R. Tolkien. Originally published in three volumes between 1954 and 1955, "The Lord of the Rings" is one of the most beloved and influential works of fantasy literature. The story is set in the fictional world of Middle-earth and follows the quest to destroy the One Ring, a powerful artifact created by the dark lord Sauron to conquer and rule all of Middle-earth. The protagonist, Frodo Baggins, along with his companions, including the wizard Gandalf, the ranger Aragorn, the dwarf Gimli, the elf Legolas, and others, undertake a perilous journey to Mount Doom, the only place where the Ring can be destroyed.

10.gone girl

gillian flynn

description:

Gone Girl" is a psychological thriller novel written by American author Gillian Flynn, published in 2012. The story revolves around the mysterious disappearance of Amy Elliott Dunne on her fifth wedding anniversary. As the investigation unfolds, suspicion falls on Amy's husband, Nick Dunne, who becomes the prime suspect in her disappearance. The narrative alternates between the perspectives of Nick and Amy, revealing their troubled marriage and the secrets they each hold. As the search for Amy intensifies, the media frenzy surrounding the case escalates, exposing the cracks in Nick and Amy's relationship and raising questions about their true intentions. "Gone Girl" is acclaimed for its gripping plot, unpredictable twists, and complex characters. Flynn masterfully explores themes of deception, manipulation, and the nature of truth, keeping readers on the edge of their seats until the shocking conclusion.
